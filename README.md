## ðŸ§  Pathway Overviews

### Nigrostriatal Pathway
- **Origin:** Substantia nigra pars compacta (SNc)  
- **Target:** Dorsal striatum (caudateâ€“putamen)  
- **Function:** Voluntary movement, action selection, habit formation  
- **Clinical relevance:** Degeneration of SNc neurons is a hallmark of **Parkinsonâ€™s disease**, causing bradykinesia, rigidity, and tremor.

### Mesolimbic Pathway
- **Origin:** Ventral tegmental area (VTA)  
- **Target:** Ventral striatum (nucleus accumbens) and limbic structures (amygdala, hippocampus)  
- **Function:** Reward, motivation, reinforcement learning  
- **Clinical relevance:** Implicated in addiction, hedonic tone, and some positive symptoms of schizophrenia.

### Mesocortical Pathway
- **Origin:** Ventral tegmental area (VTA)  
- **Target:** Prefrontal cortex (especially medial PFC)  
- **Function:** Executive function, working memory, decision-making, cognitive flexibility  
- **Clinical relevance:** Dysregulation associated with cognitive and negative symptoms in psychiatric disorders.
